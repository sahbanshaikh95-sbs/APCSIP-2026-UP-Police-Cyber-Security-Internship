# OSINT & Information Intelligence

## Overview

This session focused on **Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT)** and **Information Intelligence**. Various techniques and tools used to gather publicly available information for cybersecurity investigations, threat intelligence, and digital investigations were introduced.

## Topics Covered

### Google Dorking

Learned how advanced Google search operators can be used to discover publicly available information more efficiently.

Examples include:

* `site:`
* `filetype:`
* `intitle:`
* `inurl:`

### Shodan

Introduced to Shodan, a search engine used to discover internet-connected devices, services, and exposed systems.

Key uses:

* Asset discovery
* Service enumeration
* Exposure assessment

### Phone Number OSINT

Explored tools used for gathering publicly available information related to phone numbers.

Tools discussed:

* PhoneInfoga
* PhoneInfo
* Find & Trace

### Email OSINT

Learned methods for investigating email addresses using open-source intelligence techniques.

Tools discussed:

* Phonebook.cz
* DeHashed

### Metadata Analysis

Learned how metadata can reveal useful information from files and images.

Tool discussed:

* Jimpl

### Reverse Image Intelligence

Explored image investigation techniques using:

* Yandex Reverse Image Search
* Lenzo AI

### Domain & Infrastructure Intelligence

Learned about:

* crt.sh

  * Certificate Transparency Logs
  * Subdomain discovery
  * Infrastructure reconnaissance

### Geolocation Intelligence

Discussed tools and techniques used for geolocation investigations.

Tools discussed:

* GeoOSINT

### Tracking & Awareness Demonstrations

Introduced to:

* Grabify (IP logging and tracking demonstrations)
* Canary Tokens (tripwire and alerting mechanisms)

## Key Learning

A major takeaway from the session was understanding the distinction between:

### Legal OSINT

Information that is publicly available and accessible through legitimate sources.

Examples:

* Search engines
* Public records
* Social media profiles
* Certificate transparency logs

### Illegal or Restricted Data Sources

Data originating from unauthorized disclosures or breaches may have legal and ethical implications.

Examples:

* Leaked databases
* Unauthorized access to private information

## Key Takeaways

* OSINT plays a critical role in cybersecurity investigations.
* Publicly available information can reveal significant intelligence.
* Metadata can expose sensitive information unintentionally.
* Email, phone number, image, and domain investigations are important OSINT techniques.
* Ethical and legal considerations must always be followed during intelligence gathering.

## Reflection

This session provided practical exposure to the fundamentals of OSINT and Information Intelligence. It highlighted how publicly available information can be leveraged for investigations, threat analysis, and cybersecurity research while emphasizing responsible and ethical usage.
