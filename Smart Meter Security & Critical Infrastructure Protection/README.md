# Smart Meter Security & Critical Infrastructure Protection

## Overview

This session focused on cybersecurity challenges in smart energy infrastructure, smart meter attacks, and the impact of cyberattacks on critical infrastructure.

---

# What is a Smart Meter?

A smart meter is a digital device that records and transmits electricity, gas, or water consumption data.

### Features

- Real-time monitoring
- Remote communication
- Automated billing
- Usage analytics

---

# Smart Grid Ecosystem

Components include:

- Smart Meters
- Utility Providers
- Communication Networks
- Data Collection Systems
- Consumer Portals

---

# Security Challenges

## Weak Authentication

Poor authentication mechanisms can allow unauthorized access.

---

## Insecure Communication

Unencrypted communication may expose sensitive information.

---

## Firmware Vulnerabilities

Outdated firmware can contain exploitable vulnerabilities.

---

## Physical Tampering

Attackers may gain direct physical access to meters.

---

# Common Smart Meter Attacks

## Energy Theft

Manipulating consumption data to reduce billing.

---

## Data Manipulation

Changing transmitted information.

---

## Denial of Service (DoS)

Disrupting communication between meters and providers.

---

## Remote Exploitation

Exploiting software vulnerabilities to gain unauthorized control.

---

## Supply Chain Attacks

Compromising hardware or software before deployment.

---

# Swedish Energy Infrastructure Case Study

The session discussed attacks targeting energy infrastructure and the importance of protecting national critical systems.

### Lessons Learned

- Critical infrastructure is a high-value target.
- Operational Technology (OT) environments require strong security.
- Availability is often more important than confidentiality.
- Incident response readiness is essential.

---

## Financial Impact

- Revenue loss

---

## Operational Impact

---

## National Security Impact

# Securing Smart Meters

### Recommended Controls

- Strong authentication
- Secure firmware updates
- Encryption
- Network segmentation
- Continuous monitoring
- Security audits

---

# Critical Infrastructure Security

Critical sectors include:

- Energy
- Water
- Transportation
- Healthcare
- Telecommunications

Protecting these systems is a national cybersecurity priority.

---

# Key Takeaways

- Smart meters are attractive attack targets.
- Energy infrastructure attacks can have large-scale consequences.
- Security must be integrated throughout the device lifecycle.
- Critical infrastructure protection is a strategic cybersecurity challenge.
